THE FACULTY

LENNA DARBIN ANDREWS. Voice, Piano. Received her Bachelor of Music in vocal performance from Indiana University. She has taught music in schools, early childhood music programs, and private voice and piano. Ms. Andrews performs with prominent churches and synagogues, in addition to regular appearances with regional opera companies, and as a soloist with choral groups both locally and in New York City.

YVETTE BEZAHLER. Violin, Viola. B.A. in Music Education, M.A.T. Candidate Manhattanville College. Teaching Diploma in Violin and Piano from The Music School of Lausanne, Switzerland. Ms. Bezahler has taught music classes in the public school system for several years. She has been a member of orchestras in Switzerland as well as the U.S. and currently performs with Chamber Music groups in New York City.

CHARLES COSTA. Guitar.  Received his B.M. in Jazz Composition from SUNY Purchase.  He has prepared students in workshops, classes, and ensembles, many of whom went on to further study at universities.  Charlie's main focus is to encourage ear training, theory, and improvement in everyday learning.  He is an active musician and has been performing nationally with different artists for several past years.

IPEK EGINLIOUGLU. Piano.  Received her M.M. degree from SUNY Purchase on scholarship. She has performed extensively in Europe and the United States and has participated in several master classes.  Ipek has won many awards and is an experienced teacher of all ages. 

REFIKA ELIBAY. Piano. Received B.M. and M.M. degrees from Manhattan School of Music on scholarships; she received two Silver Medals at the International Piano Recording Competition and was District I winner of NYS Music Association Competition. Ms. Elibay has performed in numerous recitals in the New York City area, including performances on WNYC and Cable TV and recitals in Trinity Church.

DONALD ISLER. Piano. Received his B.M. and M.M. degrees from Manhattan School of Music.  Founder of KASP Records which produced his CDs with music of Beethoven and Schubert as well as rare works of Schnabel and Spohr.  Mr. Isler has performed at Carnegie Recital Hall and Alice Tully Hall.  He has been on the faculties of the Brooklyn College Preparatory Center and the Diller-Quaile Music School.  

JENNIFER JILES. Adult Beginner Ballet. Holds a BFA in Dance from SUNY Purchase School of the Arts.  Ms. Jiles was a Radio City Music Hall Rockette from 1990-1996.  Some of her other dance credits include:  company member of the American Dance Machine, the Red Bank Ballet, and Long Island Ballet; Sister Mary Leo in “Nunsense”; Lorraine in “42nd Street”; full scholarships at Alvin Ailey American Dance Theatre, Broadway Dance Center, and the Martha Graham School.  Ms. Jiles has studied with top ballet teachers across the country and has over 20 years professional experience in the dance, theatre, television and comedy worlds.  Currently, she is an EmmyAward-winning actress, host and voice-over artist.   www.jenniferjiles.com

STACY LABRIOLA.  Guitar. Received her B.A. from Connecticut College.  Ms. Labriola has taught early developmental music, beginning guitar (to over 50 boys and girls) at the Fresh Air Fund Camps in Wappinger Falls, New York, and continues private instruction.  She is also a registered Music Together teacher, a singer, songwriter, guitar player and founding member of MotherLode Trio, a country rock, folk group. 

JANE PARKHOUSE.  Violin.  Graduate of London University (B.Ed. Music). She has taught violin, early childhood music and coached ensembles extensively in London, New York, Washington, and Boston. In addition to performing with a variety of ensembles, Ms. Parkhouse is the founder and co-director of a summer music and drama camp in the Boston area.

YELENA SALENKO.   Voice, Piano. Received  a B.M. in Music with a major in Choral Conducting from Orenburg Musical College, Russia and an M.M. in Vocal Performance from Saratov Conservatory, Russia.  She has taught piano and conducted a chorus in the Music School for Gifted Children in Orenburg.  Ms. Salenko has been a criticallly acclaimed favorite artist with Expromt Musical Theater in Moscow for four seasons and won a nomination for the prestigious Golden Mask 97 award.

ERIKA SCHLENKERMANN.   Piano, Woodwinds.. Received her B.M. in Music Education with Performance Honors from Syracuse University.  Ms. Schlenkermann has performed in numerous recitals and concerts.  She has performed in Les Miserables at the Westchester Premier Theater and was music director and woodwind player for new-age classical composer CAL. She has also taught in several area music schools, and provides private instruction to students of all ages. 

LINDA THORNTON. Music Together (R). Bennett College, A.S. in Child Study.  Ms. Thornton studied voice, dance and musical theater in New York City at the American Music and Dramatic Academy and the International School of Dance at Carnegie Hall. A registered Music Together teacher since 1993, she is a member of the Director's Leadership Group and Mentor Team, and represented Music Together at the North American NAEYC Conference in 1998.  She was on the staff at the Purchase Children's Center for 13 years as Music Specialist and Pre-School teacher.
