CLASS INSTRUCTION


          I. Music Together (R) (ages birth to 4) with Adults.

    Developed by the Center for Music and Young Children in Princeton, New Jersey, children sing, move, chant, listen, and explore rhythm instruments at their own level with their parent or care giver. Activities focus on adult/child interaction so that siblings and children of mixed ages can enjoy class together.  Tuition includes an outstanding recording of music used in the class, a beautifully illustrated songbook, and a special publication "Music and your Child" for new parents.
Instructor: Linda Thornton
Now taught at Music on Hudson, 39 N. Riverside Ave., Croton-on-Hudson.
For more information contact Linda at 845-788-3878 or visit her website

    II. Pre-K Music Exploration and Creative Movement (ages 4-5).
    This class introduces new songs and reinforces old favorites, encouraging vocal play, improvisation and focus on rhythm.  The children will work on rhythm patterns through claps, snaps, and vocal and instrument play.  Early ear training wil be taught through tonal patterns introducing harmony.  Whole body movement and dance will be encouraged.  (ten students)
          Instructor: Stacy Labriola 
          (45 min.) 15 week term:  $275.00
          Time: Tuesday 3:45 PM    

    III. Eurythmic classes (age level groups 5-7).
    In this class the students learn the connection between musical rhythms and the natural movements of the body. This method is useful with children of all ages, since it corresponds with their natural desire to sing, dance and clap out rhythms while moving to music in a joyful manner. "Dalcroze Eurythmics" is divided into three sections: Eurythmics, Solfege, and Improvisation, and was developed by the Swiss pedagogue Emile Jaques-Dalcroze.
    (six students)
          Instructor: Refika Elibay
          (45 min.) 18 week term: $325.00
          Tuesday 4:00 PM

    IV. Rudimentary Keyboard and Expressive Movement (ages 5-7).
    Basic keyboard playing and music reading are explored, including early ear training and music theory. These young students are also encouraged to express themselves through rhythmic play, musical games, expressive movement, and echo and independent singing, leading to an early choral experience. (six to eight students)

Vocal ensemble for those who love to sing. The objective of the class is to develop musicianship skills and a love of singing through enjoyable experiences.  Our students will learn the music fundamentals of rhythm, melody, harmony, ear training and sight reading in both solo and ensemble singing. (ten students)

    VI.  Guitar Class (ages 8 and up)
In this class, students learn beginning guitar in a fun and relaxed atmosphere. Students will learn the basic chords and chord variations as well as a variety of rhythmic strum patterns, folk picks and simple melodies. They will learn to play in a band-like setting varying their melody and rhythm by playing with each other and the teacher.  By the end of session, students will be playing a variety of basic songs including traditional folk favorites, rock, and blues.  (four to six students)

                    Instructor: Charles Costa

                    Wednesday 5:00 PM

    VIII.  Music Appreciation (all ages)
Students will study selected pieces of music from different periods in history and learn how they relate to the culture in which they were created.  Listening to classical music also provides the possibility of increasing language development, creativity, coordination, and social interaction.  Previous musical training is not required to join this class.

                    Instructor: Yalena Salenko

    IX.  Adult Beginner Ballet (teens and adults of all ages)
A basic ballet technique class geared toward adult beginners with no experience necessary.  This class is also appropriate for adults and teens with any amount of ballet or dance experience who want to brush up or advance their skills. This is a great exercise class where you can get into shape and have fun doing it.  Ballet creates poise, gracefulness and long, lean, bodies.  Ballet also works all your muscles and after the first few workouts, you'll feel muscles you forgot you had! (nine to twelve students)
