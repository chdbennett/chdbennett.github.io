Section 8

The Section 8 program is a federal rent subsidy program operated by the Dept. of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) in Washington D.C. HUD channels funds to states; states distribute the funds to housing authorities at the county, state and local levels. Those agencies award the funds to people who apply and demonstrate that they qualify based on need and household income. A person who qualifies can be issued a CERTIFICATE or a VOUCHER---a document with the participant's name and an indication of the size rental unit the participant is authorized to look for. It is tangible evidence that the housing authority has found the subsidy holder eligible and that it will guarantee a large portion of the rent due on any acceptable apartment the participant chooses to rent. Unfortunately in many areas there is a large backlog of people who would meet the income and need guidlines, but who have been put on a waiting list. Many local authorities are no longer even accepting applications.
